```html
<!-- Slide 17: Two-Panel: The Danger of Automating a Bad Process -->
<div id="slide-17" class="slide">
    <div class="two-panel-wrapper">
        <div class="image-panel">
            <img src="img-information-system-today-chapter-2/slide-17.png" alt="Magnifying glass over a flawed process">
        </div>
        <div class="text-panel">
            <h1>The Danger of Automating a Bad Process</h1>
            <p>Automating without learning can be dangerous. If the underlying business process is flawed, technology will only magnify the problem.</p>
            <ul>
                <li><strong>Manual Flawed Process:</strong> A loan officer manually approves 2 "bad" loans per week.</li>
                <li><strong>Automated Flawed Process:</strong> The same faulty logic is automated, and the system now helps the officer approve 6 "bad" loans per week.</li>
            </ul>
            <p class="conclusion">Without a learning component to analyze outcomes and identify the flawed criteria, the organization is simply making mistakes faster.</p>
        </div>
    </div>
</div>
```

```html

    <div class="two-panel-wrapper image-right">
        <div class="image-panel">
            <img src="img-information-system-today-chapter-2/slide-32.png" alt="Illustration of the Freemium model">
        </div>
        <div class="text-panel">
            <h1>Freemium in Action: Dropbox</h1>
            <ul>
                <li><strong>Free Tier:</strong> Offers a small amount of cloud storage (e.g., 2GB) for free. This attracts a massive user base.</li>
                <li><strong>Premium Tier:</strong> Users who need more storage can subscribe to a paid plan with significantly more space and additional features.</li>
                <li><strong>Benefit:</strong> The large base of free users acts as a powerful marketing engine through word-of-mouth referrals.</li>
            </ul>
            <p class="conclusion">This model is extremely popular for mobile apps, games (with in-app purchases), and software-as-a-service (SaaS) products.</p>
        </div>
    </div>
</div>
```

### Slide 46: The MVP Concept

```html
<!-- Slide 46: Two-Panel: The MVP Concept -->
<div id="slide-46" class="slide">
    <div class="two-panel-wrapper image-right">
        <div class="image-panel">
            <img src="img-information-system-today-chapter-2/slide-46.png" alt="Illustration of the MVP concept">
        </div>
        <div class="text-panel">
            <h1>Visualizing the MVP</h1>
            <p>Instead of building a product piece by piece (which delivers no value until the end), the MVP approach focuses on delivering a functional, albeit basic, version at each stage.</p>
            <p>You don't build a wheel, then an axle, then a chassis. You build a skateboard, then a scooter, then a bicycle, then a car.</p>
            <p class="conclusion">Each iteration is a usable product that solves the core problem and allows you to learn from real users.</p>
        </div>
    </div>
</div>
```
